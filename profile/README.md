> **Deciphering the molecular and cellular mechanisms of cancer and aging.**

Welcome to the official GitHub organization of **GenCanAge**, a multidisciplinary research group based at the **University of Oviedo** and affiliated with **IUOPA**, **CIBERonc**, and **ISPA**. 

Founded over 25 years ago by Prof. Carlos López-Otín, our group continues a legacy of scientific excellence. We integrate the study of cellular and animal models with precision medicine and the comparative genomics of long-lived species to translate mechanistic findings into innovative diagnostic and therapeutic strategies for human health, focusing on aging and cancer research.

---

## 🔬 Our Research Highlights

### ♋ Cancer Research
Our group has a distinguished trajectory in oncology, pioneering genomic approaches to understand tumor evolution. 
* **Genomic Sequencing:** Co-led the Spanish contribution to the International Cancer Genome Consortium (ICGC), revealing the whole-genome sequence of hundreds of Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL) patients and identifying key therapeutic targets.
* **Precision Medicine:** Integrating single-cell sequencing and preclinical models to decipher tumor evolution and microenvironment dynamics, while advancing genomic diagnostics for lymphomas (ISCIII).
* **Clonal Hematopoiesis (CH):** Investigating CH as a high-risk precursor for myeloid neoplasms to uncover the biological and environmental factors driving clonal expansion and early oncological development.

### ⏳ Aging and Age-Related Diseases
We are defining the regulatory landscapes of biological decline and physiological aging.
* **Murine Models:** Developed pioneering models of accelerated aging (*Zmpste24−/−* and *LmnaG609G/G609G* mice), which serve as essential tools globally for evaluating pharmacological, metabolic, and *in vivo* gene editing interventions.
* **The Hallmarks Framework:** Proud contributors to the definition of the internationally recognized **Hallmarks of Aging** and **Hallmarks of Health**, establishing an integrative paradigm for understanding normal and pathological aging.
* **Multi-omics:** Characterizing transcriptomic and epigenomic alterations associated with premature and physiological aging.

### 🐢 Comparative Genomics
We compare the genomes of species with divergent lifespans to uncover fundamental mechanisms regulating aging, cancer, and stress resistance.
* **Exceptional Longevity:** Previous milestones include genomic analyses of the humpback whale, giant tortoises, and the "immortal jellyfish" (*Turritopsis dohrnii*), identifying critical variants in DNA repair, stem cell biology, and immune responses. Building on these milestones, we are currently performing comparative genomic and transcriptional analyses of species with evolutionary adaptations for extreme longevity or healthspan, aiming to identify novel molecular targets for promoting healthy aging.

### ♻️ Autophagy & Autosis (AU·SIS Project)
Investigating the complex, dual role of autophagy in tumor survival and cellular homeostasis.
* **Autosis Characterization:** Defining the molecular regulation of autosis, a unique, Na⁺,K⁺-ATPase-dependent cell death pathway.
* **Clinical Translation:** Identifying novel autophagic markers to improve diagnostic and therapeutic strategies for oncology and other pathologies.
